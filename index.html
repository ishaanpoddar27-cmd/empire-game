The British Empire

Industry, Colonies & Control — 1783 to 1914

Britain was the first industrial nation. To supply its factories with raw materials and sell its finished goods, it built and maintained a global empire. Explore the methods of colonial control, then visit each location to learn its history and test your knowledge :)

Part 1 Methods of Colonisation

How Britain controlled the world

Britain was a relatively small island ruling over hundreds of millions of people. It could not win by numbers alone, so it relied on technology, economic pressure, and political manipulation.

Naval Power and Gunboat Diplomacy

Britain had the most powerful navy in the world. If a local government refused to trade or tried to raise taxes on British goods for example, Britain would position a heavily armed warship in their harbour. This was called "Gunboat Diplomacy": accept British terms, or face bombardment. It was used against China, Egypt, and many others.

The Telegraph

Before the 1850s, a message from London to India, for example, took months. By the 1870s, undersea telegraph cables allowed orders to be sent in minutes. If a rebellion started anywhere in the Empire, London was informed basically immediately and could dispatch troops by steamship before the uprising could spread.

Divide and Rule

Britain identified existing ethnic, religious, or social tensions within a territory and gave power, better jobs, or education to one specific group. This created rivalry between local groups and prevented them from uniting against British rule. This strategy was used extensively in India, Africa, and the Caribbean.

Debt and Infrastructure

Britain funded the construction of railways, ports, and canals in places like Argentina, Egypt, and India, while using British loans and British engineers. The territory would accumulate large debts to British banks. To repay them, it had to continue exporting raw materials to Britain at low prices. The railways were built to move resources to ports, not to serve local populations.

Technological Superiority —> The Maxim Gun

In the late 1800s, the Maxim Gun (the world's first portable automatic machine gun) allowed small British forces to defeat much larger local armies. A famous saying of the era: "Whatever happens, we have got the Maxim Gun, and they have not." This allowed Britain to hold colonies in Africa and Asia where it was heavily outnumbered.

The Four Mechanisms of Colonial Control

Extraction: Taking raw materials (cotton, wool, oil) from a territory at very low cost.
Infrastructure: Building railways and telegraph lines to move resources to ports faster, not to develop local economies.
Captive Markets: Forcing colonies to buy only British-made goods, preventing them from developing their own industries.
Human Cost: Forced labour, displacement of Indigenous peoples, and destruction of local environments.

      Final Report

      Developments

      Steamships and telegraph cables connected the world. Britain built railways and ports across multiple continents. Industrial wealth funded medical and scientific research.

      Exploitation

      Local industries were deliberately broken to create captive markets. Forced labour and displacement of Indigenous peoples were widespread. Local environments were damaged to supply British factories.
